Recalibrate Build Prompt
========================

AI Build Prompt — "Recalibrate" Mobile App (v1.0)MVP: Configurable Habit & Behavior-Change Engine, seeded with the 8-Week Recovery & Discipline ProtocolHow to use this document: Paste this entire file into your AI coding tool (Claude Code, Cursor, etc.) as the build spec. It is written as instructions addressed to that AI. "Discipline OS" is a placeholder name — replace it freely. Where I made an assumption (tech stack, default scoring weights, etc.) it's flagged so you can override it.1. Mission & Product PhilosophyYou are building a mobile app that helps people change specific behaviors — quitting or reducing smoking, drinking, compulsive sexual behavior, or any other habit they name — by combining four things that actually have evidence behind them: structured self-monitoring, environment design, identity-based habit change, and short daily action over willpower.Ground every feature in real behavior-change science, not motivational hype:Self-monitoring (Bandura's self-regulation theory): the act of tracking a behavior changes the behavior, independent of any advice given. This is why a configurable tracker is the foundation, not an add-on.Habit loop (cue → routine → reward) and environment design: behavior follows context more reliably than it follows motivation. This is why Week 2 of the seed program is about removing triggers and adding friction, not about willpower.Implementation intentions (Gollwitzer): "if X, then I will do Y" plans outperform vague goals. Daily tasks should be phrased this concretely wherever possible.Identity-based habit change (built on self-perception theory): people sustain behaviors that match their self-image. This is why Week 5 tracks "did I act like the person I'm becoming" rather than just task completion.Mindfulness-Based Relapse Prevention (Bowen & Witkiewitz): a slip is data, not a moral failure. The app's tone after a missed day or a relapse must be curious and corrective, never punitive.Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan): motivation that's autonomous (the user's own reasons) lasts; motivation that's purely extrinsic (badges, streak fear) decays and can crowd out the user's intrinsic motivation. Gamification in this app should support autonomy and competence, not replace them.Motivational Interviewing (Miller & Rollnick): the in-app AI coach should ask, reflect, and summarize far more than it instructs.Marketing/voice rule: never use language like "ancient secrets," "guaranteed discipline," "hack your dopamine," or "biohack." This is a structured behavior-change tool. Say that plainly. It's more credible and it's what the science actually supports.2. Target Users & Ethical GuardrailsTarget users: adults who want to reduce a specific behavior (smoking, drinking, compulsive porn/masturbation use, doomscrolling, etc.) and/or build new ones (exercise, focus, sleep), using a structured multi-week program plus daily tracking.Hard constraints — bake these into the spec, not just the marketing copy:This is not a medical device and not a substitute for therapy or addiction treatment. Include this in onboarding and in settings, and the AI coach must repeat it when relevant.Crisis handling: if journal or chat text suggests suicidal ideation, self-harm, or a medical emergency (e.g., withdrawal symptoms, blackouts), the app must immediately surface region-appropriate crisis resources (e.g., 988 in the US, Samaritans in the UK, or a locale-detected equivalent) rather than continue coaching or scoring that entry. Don't make absolute claims about confidentiality of this data — just be reliable about surfacing the resource.Don't over-pathologize. "Porn/masturbation" tracking should be framed as a behavior this specific user wants to change, not as a diagnosed addiction — "porn addiction" and "sex addiction" aren't DSM-5 diagnoses (the closest recognized category, ICD-11's Compulsive Sexual Behaviour Disorder, is narrow and relatively rare). Default copy should be neutral ("Sexual behavior — per your goal") and the app should never auto-label a user as "addicted" anywhere in the UI.No shame-based copy, ever. A missed day is "missed," not "failed." A relapse triggers a "Reset Flow" (Section 4.8), never a punitive label or red warning screen.Sensitive data protection: any tracker the user marks sensitive (defaults: smoking, alcohol, sexual behavior) is excluded from any future community/sharing feature by default, opt-in only, and can be hidden behind a secondary biometric lock within the app.No dark patterns. This app's entire purpose is helping people regulate compulsive engagement with their phone — it would be self-defeating to use infinite scroll, variable-ratio reward notifications, or guilt-trip push copy ("Don't break your streak!! 😱") to keep people in-app. Notifications should be calm, infrequent, and dismissible without penalty.3. Information Architecture (Core Data Models)Everything below is user-configurable data, not hardcoded logic. The 8-week program in Section 7 ships as seed rows in these tables, not as special-cased code.User
 - id, auth info, locale, timezone
 - settings: theme, score_weights, biometric_lock_metrics[], notification_prefs

Program                      // a multi-week plan (the "8-Week Protocol" is one row)
 - id, title, description, author, length_in_weeks, is_active_for_user
 - created_by ("system" | user_id)   // so users can build their own later

ProgramWeek
 - id, program_id, week_number, theme, goal_text, psychology_rationale (optional, shown via "why this?" tooltip)

DailyTask
 - id, program_week_id (nullable — can also be global), title, type (checkbox | linked_metric), frequency, sort_order

TrackedMetric                // THE configurable tracking engine
 - id, user_id, name, icon, category ("build" | "reduce" | "neutral")
 - input_type ("boolean" | "counter" | "scale_1_10" | "duration_min" | "custom_unit")
 - unit_label (nullable, e.g. "cigarettes", "units", "min")
 - direction ("higher_is_better" | "lower_is_better" | "track_only")
 - is_sensitive (bool) -> excluded from sharing, eligible for extra lock
 - include_in_score (bool)          // see Section 4.5 — default FALSE for mood/stress
 - reminder_times[]
 - is_system_default (bool)         // seeded vs user-created

MetricEntry (a.k.a. DailyLog)
 - id, user_id, metric_id, date, value, note (optional)

JournalEntry
 - id, user_id, date, prompt_id (nullable, freeform allowed), text, mood, energy, stress
   // mood/energy/stress live HERE, not as scored metrics — see 4.5

ScoreConfig
 - id, user_id, weights{} (JSON, user-editable), formula_version

Streak
 - id, user_id, metric_id OR task_id, current_count, longest_count, last_completed_date, grace_days_used

ResetFlowLog               // replaces "relapse log" — see 4.8
 - id, user_id, metric_id, date, trigger_reflection, plan_adjustment

Reminder
 - id, user_id, linked_metric_or_task_id, time, days_of_week[], enabled
The key design decision: Program, ProgramWeek, DailyTask, and TrackedMetric are all rows a user (or you, the team, as created_by: "system") can create, edit, duplicate, or delete through the same UI. The 8-week plan isn't special — it's just the first program seeded into this schema. This is what makes "add more plans later" and "let me configure my own habits" the same engineering work, done once.4. MVP Feature SetTrue MVP (build first) vs. Phase 2 — be honest about scopeThe original concept (Stripe, admin panel, public community, full gamification, AI coach, multi-program marketplace) is closer to a V2 product than a buildable MVP. Recommend shipping:MVP (v1): Auth + onboarding, configurable Tracking Engine, one seeded Program (8-Week Protocol) with daily tasks, Daily Dashboard, objective-only Discipline Score, Mood/Journal, basic reminders, Reset Flow, privacy/data export-delete settings, a simple rule-based (non-LLM) daily insight instead of a full AI coach.Phase 2: Full LLM AI Coach, Program Builder UI for user-created programs, gamification/XP, anonymous community, admin panel, payments.The data model above already supports Phase 2 without rework — you're just not building those screens yet.4.1 OnboardingAsk what the user wants to build and what they want to reduce, in their own words. Map free text to suggested TrackedMetric rows (editable before saving — never auto-commit guesses). Set a wake time, bedtime target, and starting Program (only one choice in MVP: the 8-Week Protocol, but the picker UI should already look like a list, anticipating more programs).4.2 Configurable Tracking EngineScreen: "My Trackers." Add tracker → name, icon, category (build/reduce/neutral), input type, unit, direction, sensitive toggle, include-in-score toggle, reminder. Seed seven default trackers from the protocol (sleep hours, cigarettes, alcohol units, sexual behavior, steps, deep work minutes, parent calls) — all editable/deletable, none hardcoded.4.3 Program Engine"Programs" tab shows active program with current week highlighted, week theme, goal, and the day's tasks as a checklist. Daily non-negotiables (Section 6 of the source plan) render as a persistent, always-visible checklist regardless of which week the user is on. Implement the "never miss twice" rule as a configurable setting (default: on) that simply flags a missed-task day as a soft warning, not a punishment, on day 2.4.4 Daily DashboardToday's non-negotiables, today's program tasks, Discipline Score, current streaks, one-tap mood check-in. Keep it to a single screen, no scrolling past the fold for the primary actions — this is opened many times a day and needs to be fast.4.5 Discipline Score Engine — deliberately changed from the source specCalculate from objective, behavioral inputs only:score = (non_negotiables_completion_% * w1)
      + (program_tasks_completion_% * w2)
      + (reduce_metrics_adherence_% * w3)   // actual vs. user-set target, not vs. zero
      + (build_metrics_completion_% * w4)
default weights: w1=0.3, w2=0.3, w3=0.25, w4=0.15  (user-editable in ScoreConfig)
Do not include mood, energy, or stress in this formula. Scoring subjective emotional state creates an incentive to mask bad days, which undermines the journal's clinical value and the user's honesty with themselves. Mood/energy/stress are tracked for trend and correlation insights only ("your focus minutes are highest on days you slept 7+ hours") — never as a score input. Show the formula to the user in plain language; an opaque score erodes trust and autonomy.4.6 Mood, Energy, Stress & JournalThree 1–10 sliders plus a daily prompt rotation (what went well / what triggered me / what would I do differently). Make prompts editable per program, since different programs may want different reflection questions.4.7 Deep Work / Focus TimerSimple Pomodoro (default 25/5, configurable), logs focus_minutes to a TrackedMetric automatically rather than a separate system, so it shows up in the same trend charts as everything else.4.8 Reset Flow (replaces "Emergency Recovery / Relapse Mode")Triggered when a user logs a "reduce" metric over their target, or taps "I had a setback" directly. Flow: (1) a brief self-compassion line, not an apology demand from the user; (2) a trigger reflection ("what was happening right before?" — multiple choice + freeform, feeding CBT-style trigger pattern data over time); (3) one concrete adjustment to tomorrow's plan. Logged to ResetFlowLog, never shown as a red/failure state on the dashboard.4.9 Insights & AnalyticsTrend charts per metric, plus 2–3 surfaced correlations per week (sleep vs. mood, exercise vs. craving-metric, social contact vs. mood) using simple correlation, not causal claims in the copy.4.10 AI Coach (Phase 2 — included here so the architecture is ready)This feature needs its own system prompt. Suggested starting point for the in-app coach:You are a supportive behavior-change coach inside a personal habit app. You are not a therapist, doctor, or addiction counselor, and you say so if asked directly. Use motivational interviewing style: ask open questions, reflect back what the user said in your own words, affirm effort over outcome, and summarize periodically. Reference the user's actual tracked data when relevant, but never diagnose, never claim a clinical addiction label, and never give medical advice (e.g., about withdrawal, medication, or detox). If the user discloses anything suggesting a medical emergency, suicidal ideation, or a pattern that sounds like severe substance dependence (withdrawal symptoms, blackouts, inability to function), stop coaching that thread and direct them to the in-app crisis/professional-help resource screen. Never use shame, guilt, or fear-based language, including about streaks.4.11 Gamification (Phase 2, design carefully)If implemented: streaks should have a small number of "grace days" per month rather than resetting to zero on one miss — this protects autonomy and avoids the all-or-nothing thinking that causes people to abandon habit apps entirely after a single bad day. Avoid XP/leveling mechanics that turn the app itself into the kind of variable-reward loop the user is trying to reduce elsewhere in their life.4.12 Reminders & NotificationsPer-tracker, user-set times. Default copy is calm and informational ("Evening check-in is ready"), never urgency- or guilt-based.4.13 Privacy & Data ControlsSettings screen: export all data (JSON), delete all data, biometric lock for sensitive trackers, local-only mode if backend isn't ready for v1 (SQLite on-device is a reasonable MVP starting point — see Section 6).5. UI/UX Design DirectionMobile-first, one-handed use. Primary actions (check a task, log a metric) reachable with a thumb without scrolling.Calm visual language. Dark mode default, muted palette, generous whitespace — this is a tool used during moments of low willpower; it shouldn't compete for stimulation. Avoid saturated reds for "missed" states; use a neutral grey instead of red for non-completion.Microcopy rule: every string that responds to a missed day, a logged "reduce" metric over target, or a low mood score must be written and reviewed against the no-shame rule in Section 2. Quick test: "would a clinician be comfortable saying this to a patient?"Accessibility: support dynamic text sizing, full screen-reader labels on charts (don't rely on color alone to convey status), minimum 4.5:1 contrast.No addictive engagement patterns. No infinite scroll, no slot-machine-style streak animations, no notification badges designed to create anxiety about unread state.Core screens for MVP: Onboarding → Dashboard (home) → Program (current week + tasks) → Trackers (log + manage) → Journal/Mood → Insights → Settings/Privacy.6.
